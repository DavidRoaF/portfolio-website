PetPal

Role

UI/UX designer

Tools

Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, Sketch App and Procreate

PetPal is a project made as a part of my CareerFoundry UI design process. The objective was to solve a specific user problem while utilizing iOS design guidelines on the process.

Problem

The process of pet adoption always represents a challenge. Either if you are a first time owner or an experienced one, there is always a lack of information about breeds, shelters and pet’s status.

Solution

To create an app that will gather information about all the shelters nearby and the pets they have out for adoption, and give users information on breeds to help them make a more thoughtful decision.

My process

My first approach was to understand the problems and obstacles that users have when they are in the process of pet adoption. Moreover, I wanted to create a solution for pet adoption process that would benefit both the user and the animals. I started doing a competitive analysis on four pet adoption apps, this helped me identify needs that were unmet. After my research and interviews I was able to outline my user stories and create a user flow diagram.

With the help of the user flow diagram I defined the navigation concept for my app and started sketching my low fidelity wireframes using the crazy 8’s process. After running a usability test with 3 participants, I used their feedback to better the navigation and moved on to create the mid-fidelity wireframes.

The mid-fidelity wireframes defined all the elements of the app, their position, and structure. This layout gave me the grounds to start designing the logo, picking a typography, and picking a palette to define my brand and graphic guidelines.

Picking the right palette was the first challenge to define my brand. I decided to use a palette extracted from nature which translated in earthy-pastel colors, and gave the user a sense of relaxation and calmness.

The logo is a representation of a tag. Even though, not every pet wears a tag, but this common image is a commonplace symbol for animal companions.

Lastly, I picked my typography. I picked “SF pro text” and “SF pro display” in order to follow iOS guidelines and give the text the correct use described by apple. The typography “Baloo” was a personal pick to contrast “SF pro”, and create a more approachable brand.

Conclusion

For this project I decided to use a pastel palette, which is not the kind of palette I regularly pick for my projects. After working with it and a trial and error process, I was able to create a balanced enticing app with the colors I chose.

The navigation was clear and intuitive thanks to the initial research and interviews. This process allowed me to draw a clear path of navigation and define the elements my app would be composed of.

I met all the iOS guidelines for design a created a cohesive product.

Room for improvement

I would have liked to run usability tests with participants that have gone through the pet adoption process. This way, the feedback would have had more insight on the experience of pet adoption, rather than the design and navigation. For next iterations, I would like to explore the possibility of giving users the chance to find and fill out all the necessary paperwork for the adoption process.
